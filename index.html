gradient -- /ˈɡreɪdɪənt/

noun: gradient; plural noun: gradients

  1. an inclined part of a road or railway; a slope. "fail-safe brakes for use on steep gradients" Similar: slope incline hill rise rising ground bank ramp acclivity drop descent declivity grade the degree of a slope. "the path becomes very rough as the gradient increases" Similar: steepness angle slant slope inclination leaning MATHEMATICS the degree of steepness of a graph at any point.
  2. PHYSICS an increase or decrease in the magnitude of a property (e.g. temperature, pressure, or concentration) observed in passing from one point or moment to another. the rate of such a change. MATHEMATICS the vector formed by the operator ∇ acting on a scalar function at a given point in a scalar field.
